São Paulo - The city that never sleeps

Also known as Terra da Garoa, Sampa (São Paulo's nickname) is the largest city in Brazil with 12.2 million inhabitants. It has almost everything you could want on a business or leisure trip. There are many parks around the city, as well as endless options for leisure and culture. It doesn't matter if you prefer cultural tours, museums and theaters, or if you want to do a lot of shopping and taste food from all over the world. São Paulo will not disappoint you!

Everyday Portuguese Expressions To Know Before You Go To Brazil

Legal

Literally: “Legal”

Legal (lay-gow) literally means “legal”, but it is used to say that things are cool or awesome. Did you travel during your holidays?Legal! Some other forms to say cool that are slightly less formal: maneiro, da hora, top, sinistro.

E ai?

Literally: “And there?“

E ai (ee-eye-ee) which means “What’s up?” It is used as a way to greet friends, often followed by a Tudo bem? or Beleza?.

Eita

Literally: No translation (sound, exclamation)

Eita (eh-ta) isn’t a word that you will find in textbooks, but when you’re surrounded by Brazilians, you’ll hear it all the time. The sound expresses either fear or satisfaction, depending on the situation.

Valeu

Literally: “It was worth it.”

Valeu (val-ay-o) comes from the verb valer (“to be worth, value”). It is an informal way of saying obrigado/a (“thank you”). Sometimes you can hear Brazilians combining the two: Valeu, obrigado!

Beleza?

Literally: Beauty?

In most of the Portuguese textbooks, we learn to greet someone in Brazil, saying Tudo bem? (“All good?”), but Brazilians are way more creative than this. So instead of asking if everything is going well, they ask if everything is beautiful.

There is another expression commonly used, Tudo jóia? which literally means “All jewelry?” So next time you greet a Brazilian, ask Tudo jóia? ou Beleza? Instead of Tudo bem?
